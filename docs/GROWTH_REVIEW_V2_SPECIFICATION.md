# Purpose

The 90-Day Growth Review is the primary paid client deliverable of the Global Concepts Media Operating System.

The review must transform publicly observable business evidence into a clear consulting diagnosis, prioritized growth plan, measurable implementation roadmap, and professional client-facing deliverable.

The review must feel substantially more valuable than its $299 price.

It must not read like an AI summary, automated audit, or generic marketing report.

It must read like the work of an experienced growth consultant who understood the business, verified the evidence, identified the most important Growth Leak, and created a practical plan for the next 90 days.

# Core Consulting Method

## Find the Leak. Fix the Leak. Prove the Result.

The structure of the review must follow the Global Concepts Media consulting method.

### Find the Leak

- Understand the business.
- Classify its observable maturity.
- Review the Five Business Growth Questions™.
- Identify and prioritize the Growth Leaks.
- Show the evidence.
- Explain why the primary leak matters.

### Fix the Leak

- Define the first priority.
- Rank recommendations by impact, urgency, effort, and business fit.
- Build a practical 30/60/90-day roadmap.
- Identify implementation dependencies.
- Separate immediate corrections from larger strategic work.

### Prove the Result

- Define baseline measurements.
- Identify success metrics.
- Connect recommendations to observable business outcomes.
- Establish a review cycle.
- Avoid unsupported revenue promises.

# Core Principles

## Evidence Before Assumptions

Every conclusion must connect to observable evidence or be clearly marked for verification.

## Diagnose Before Recommending

The business must be classified before recommendations are selected.

## Recommendations Must Match Business Maturity

A startup, local business, regional brand, multi-location company, ecommerce business, professional services firm, and market leader must not receive the same recommendations.

## One Primary Growth Leak

The review may identify multiple Growth Leaks, but it must clearly identify the single highest-priority Growth Leak.

## Business Impact Before Marketing Activity

The report must explain why a finding matters to customer behavior, lead generation, conversion, trust, visibility, measurement, or revenue potential before recommending work.

## Prove the Result

Every major recommendation must include a measurable success indicator.

# Required Review Structure

## 1. Cover Page

The cover page must include:

- Global Concepts Media branding
- Client business name
- Review title
- Industry
- Geographic market
- Review period
- Prepared date
- Evidence-based positioning statement

Recommended title:

**[Business Name] — 90-Day Growth Review**

Recommended subtitle:

**Evidence-based priorities, business impact, and a practical 90-day action plan.**

---

## 2. Executive Brief

The Executive Brief must answer:

- What kind of business is being evaluated?
- What does the observable evidence indicate?
- What is the current growth position?
- What is the primary Growth Leak?
- What should happen first?
- What must be verified?

The Executive Brief must be concise enough for an owner or executive to understand the review without reading every page.

Required summary cards:

- Business Health
- Business Classification
- Current Growth Stage
- Highest-Priority Growth Leak
- First 30-Day Action

The narrative must not repeat the same recommendation in multiple forms.

---

## 3. Business Classification

The review must display the complete classification in a client-friendly format.

Required classifications:

- Business Profile
- Business Maturity
- Digital Maturity
- Trust Maturity
- Customer Journey Maturity
- Marketing Sophistication
- Growth Stage
- Appropriate Recommendation Level
- Confidence

Each classification must include:

- Classification
- Supporting evidence
- Confidence
- Verification requirements where needed

The section must explain why the later recommendations fit this business.

---

## 4. The Five Business Growth Questions™

The review must evaluate:

1. Can customers find the business?
2. Do customers understand what the business offers?
3. Do customers trust the business?
4. Can customers easily become customers?
5. Can the business prove what creates growth?

Each question must include:

- Status
- Observable evidence
- What appears strong
- What appears weak or unverified
- Business impact
- Recommended response
- Success measure

Allowed status language should be clear and professional.

Examples:

- Strong
- Developing
- Needs Attention
- Requires Verification
- Not Measurable Yet

The paid review must contain materially more reasoning than the free Snapshot.

---

## 5. Growth Leak Analysis

The review must identify:

- One Primary Growth Leak
- Up to four Supporting Growth Leaks

Each Growth Leak must include:

- Leak category
- Finding
- Observable evidence
- Why it matters
- Likely business impact
- Priority
- Estimated effort
- Recommended action
- Expected outcome
- Verification required
- Success measure

The Primary Growth Leak must be selected using:

- Business classification
- Business maturity
- Growth stage
- Evidence strength
- Business impact
- Urgency
- Implementation effort
- Dependency on other work

Generic recommendations that do not fit the business must be excluded.

---

## 6. Evidence Review

The report must show the evidence behind the diagnosis.

Evidence may include:

- Website observations
- Service and location content
- Contact pathways
- Calls to action
- Reviews and trust signals
- Credentials
- Project examples
- Leadership information
- Public profile presence
- Social activity
- Google Business Profile information
- Search and technical findings
- Measurement availability
- Missing information

Every evidence item must be labeled as:

- Confirmed
- Observable
- Requires Verification
- Not Available

The report must clearly separate evidence from assumptions.

---

## 7. Business Impact Analysis

The report must connect findings to business consequences.

Possible impact areas:

- Search visibility
- Local market relevance
- Customer trust
- Offer clarity
- Contact friction
- Lead quality
- Conversion rate
- Sales readiness
- Lead attribution
- Marketing efficiency
- Market expansion
- Competitive differentiation
- Revenue measurement

The report must not promise a specific financial return unless the required business data has been verified.

When financial data is unavailable, the report should state what must be measured before revenue impact can be estimated responsibly.

---

## 8. Prioritized Recommendations

Each recommendation must include:

- Recommendation title
- Growth Leak addressed
- Reason for priority
- Observable evidence
- Expected business impact
- Effort
- Timing
- Dependencies
- Recommended owner
- Success measure

Recommendations must be ranked.

Recommended labels:

- Immediate Priority
- High-Value Improvement
- Strategic Project
- Later Consideration

The report must distinguish between:

- Verification
- Correction
- Optimization
- Expansion
- Measurement

---

## 9. 90-Day Roadmap

The roadmap must be organized into three phases.

### Days 1–30 — Verify and Correct

Purpose:

- Confirm the primary diagnosis
- Establish baselines
- Correct high-impact low-effort issues
- Remove immediate customer friction
- Prepare larger work

Each action must include:

- Action
- Reason
- Owner
- Dependency
- Success measure

### Days 31–60 — Strengthen and Build

Purpose:

- Improve trust, visibility, conversion, or customer experience
- Implement medium-effort priorities
- Strengthen supporting systems
- Begin measuring change

### Days 61–90 — Optimize and Prove

Purpose:

- Complete strategic improvements
- Evaluate performance
- Refine based on results
- Identify the next growth cycle

The roadmap must not contain filler tasks merely to create a 90-day timeline.

---

## 10. Measurement Plan

The report must define how progress will be proven.

Required measurement categories:

- Baseline
- Primary conversion event
- Lead volume
- Lead quality
- Conversion rate
- Booked opportunities
- Close rate where available
- Average customer or project value where available
- Search visibility
- Profile engagement
- Customer actions
- Revenue contribution where verified

Every major recommendation must connect to at least one measurable outcome.

The report must identify missing tracking requirements.

---

## 11. Expected Outcomes

Expected outcomes must be expressed responsibly.

Allowed language:

- Expected to improve
- Intended to reduce
- May increase
- Creates the conditions for
- Provides a measurable baseline
- Should be evaluated using

Disallowed language without verified support:

- Guaranteed revenue
- Guaranteed ranking
- Guaranteed lead volume
- Guaranteed conversion increase
- Unsupported ROI estimates

---

## 12. Verification Requirements

The report must contain a dedicated list of information that could not be confirmed publicly.

Examples:

- Current lead volume
- Conversion rate
- Close rate
- Average project value
- Marketing budget
- Customer acquisition cost
- Analytics configuration
- Advertising performance
- Sales follow-up process
- Operational capacity
- Profitability
- Market share

Verification requirements must not be presented as defects.

They are information needed to improve confidence and measurement.

---

## 13. Implementation Options

The report must present clear implementation paths.

### Internal Implementation

The client assigns the roadmap to its internal team.

### Global Concepts Media Implementation

Global Concepts Media verifies the priorities, completes approved work, and measures results.

### Qualified Third-Party Implementation

The client uses another qualified provider while retaining the review as the implementation standard.

### Ongoing Growth Partnership

Global Concepts Media manages phased execution, measurement, and continuous improvement.

This section must remain consultative rather than overly promotional.

---

## 14. Final Consultant Recommendation

The report must end with a concise professional recommendation.

It should answer:

- What should happen first?
- Why should it happen first?
- What should not happen yet?
- What must be measured?
- What decision should the client make next?

Recommended closing structure:

```text
First:
[Immediate action]

Then:
[Supporting work]

Do not:
[Premature or inappropriate action]

Measure:
[Primary success indicator]

Next decision:
[Implementation choice]
```

# Free Snapshot Versus Paid Growth Review

## Free Business Snapshot

The free Snapshot may include:

- Observable business profile
- High-level readiness
- Strongest visible asset
- One visible opportunity
- One recommended first action
- High-level Five Questions status
- Areas requiring deeper investigation

The free Snapshot must not include:

- Complete business classification reasoning
- Full evidence review
- All Growth Leaks
- Detailed prioritization
- Complete recommendation sequence
- Full 90-day roadmap
- Detailed measurement plan
- Implementation dependencies
- Complete success metrics
- Consultant-reviewed final recommendation

## Paid 90-Day Growth Review

The paid review must include the full diagnosis, evidence, business impact, prioritization, roadmap, measurement plan, and implementation guidance.

The difference between free and paid must be unmistakable.
